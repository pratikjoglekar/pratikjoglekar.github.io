HubSpot Inc.

Sept 2022 - Present

Usage Library —

Website

Previously, HubSpot employees lacked a method to organize and monitor events using the data HubSpot possesses about their customers in an organized manner. With the Usage Library, HubSpot employees can now view all customer-related events and have it available for both amplitude and other analytical platforms. This tool also allows teams to establish uniform data practices, safeguard the information, and ensure its accuracy.

My Role —

My job was to design the end-to-end experience. I focused on how events are created and changed, and how this affects analytical tools. Along with that I also created a designs to better structure data for accessibility. One of my main goals was to make sure all customer data was safe, reliable, and easy to use.

Usage Library: Event Creation Flow —

Website

The usage library was mainly used to store all events. However, there was a big problem with creating events. There was no way to track or record when events were created or edited. To fix this, I suggested making a system that used a database. Users could easily create events through the interface instead of using a code file.

My Role —

The primary objective of my efforts was to understand the current system and identify areas for enhancement. It became evident that we required a framework for creating and modifying events. To ensure the success of the tool, I extensively researched through interviews and usability studies the user expectations and translated them into actionable designs.

Usage Library: Advance Anomaly Detection —

Website

The system employs machine learning to detect any irregularities in the data, enabling teams to detect and address critical issues at an earlier stage. The majority of the data holds significance for the company, and the teams can set up event configurations to pinpoint particular occurrences they wish to monitor.

My Role —

Worked closely with engineers and the ML team to pinpoint potential for improved anomaly detection. Developed a design that considered both technical capabilities and business needs. Subsequently, I carried out a usability analysis to fine-tune and solidify the designs for implementation.

Skill Highlights —

  • System Thinking: Developed a comprehensive system flow for the project to assess the methods of data generation and processing. Additionally, determined the user flow for event utilization and integration with analytics tools.
  • Influence: Suggested ways to manage the project and set a plan, by understanding both the technical limits and the business goals.
  • Teamwork: ADue to the highly technical nature of this project, involving engineers earlier in the process enabled me to discover valuable insights and determine a strategy.
  • Research: Conducted interviews with analysts and engineers to gain insight into their needs and incorporate them into the event library, organizing them effectively.
  • Collaboration & Stakeholder management: In order to determine the most effective course of action and obtain necessary approvals, I collaborated with various stakeholders and initiated discussions. Through team deliberations and additional brainstorming, I was able to efficiently solidify designs.

User Engagement —

Website

Due to their vulnerability to market changes, small businesses are especially sensitive to pricing. In fact, 25% of customers opt to cancel their automatic renewal in order to save money. However, it takes HubSpot 2 years to recoup the cost of acquiring a customer. In order to retain these customers, we incentivized them with a discount during the cancellation process.

Additionally, we took into account their ongoing engagement with the product and aimed to introduce them to new tools and features or remind them about ones they may have forgotten about.

My Role —

In order to address legal and pricing concerns, I had to work closely with both the legal and fintech departments. Additionally, I partnered with the analyst to gather data for an informed decision and devised a plan that would enable users to take advantage of discounts while still utilizing HubSpot products.

In order to involve users, I implemented a variety of growth initiatives aimed at further engaging them with the product. These efforts involved conducting experiments to determine the most effective prompts for targeted user engagement.

Skill Highlights —

  • Strategy: Created a thorough design strategy to implement a 0-to-1 design experience for prompts and cancellation flow discount
  • Lead: Spearheaded the entire design effort for the product from inception, which included talking with stakeholders, user validations, internal working agreements, capacity planning, etc.
  • Influence: Worked closely with the product and engineering team to constantly remind and connect any experience to user insights.
  • Understand Tech and Legal Expectations: Learning a few of the legal and compliance expectations to foresee any recommendations and collaborated with engineering to overcome legacy hurdles.
  • Collaboration: Partnered with the product owner and engineering to work towards roadmap and technical constraints identification.
